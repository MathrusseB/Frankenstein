01
Principles

How the brand
should feel online.

Five rules that decide every design choice downstream. When something feels off, one of these is broken.

01 / Weight

Heavy over light.

This is iron. Thin type, glassmorphism, soft gradients all lie about the product. Condensed display. Dense layouts. Hard edges. Zero radii above 4px.

02 / Contrast

Hard light.

Near-black backgrounds. Warm off-white foregrounds. Photography lit like a shop — hard rim light, deep shadows. No moody grey mid-tones.

03 / Voice

Frank talks, we listen.

Keep the founder's voice. Owner's Promise stays. Monospace labels frame the content like a spec sheet. No marketing boilerplate.

04 / Evidence

Show the iron.

Every kit, every component, a photo — not an icon. Dana diffs, 9" axles, billet calipers: those are the hero shots. If we don't have a photo, we say so out loud.

05 / One loud color

Monster green, everywhere.

A single accent — Frankenstein acid green — for CTAs, active states, key numbers. No rainbow chart palettes. Red only for warnings or shock CTAs.

06 / Restraint

One interaction that pays off.

Skip the animations parade. Land one thing: the Kit Finder (Year → Make → Model). That's the only "wow" moment the site needs.

Usage rules

/ Don't bend these
DO
  • Use Monster on ONE thing per view: the primary CTA, or a single data point.
  • Default every page to Ink background. Paper backgrounds only for "Our Story" / long-form.
  • Hairline rules (1px Line) between sections instead of boxes and drop shadows.
  • Monospace for numbers, specs, labels, and timestamps — never for body copy.
DON'T
  • Tint buttons or cards with Monster green. It's not a fill color, it's a highlighter.
  • Gradients. Ever. This is paint, not plasma.
  • Rounded corners above 4px. It turns the site into a SaaS dashboard.
  • More than two accents on a page. The whole point is one shot of green.

06
Interaction · Kit Finder

One interaction
that earns its keep.

The single "wow" moment on the site. Year → Make → Model → matching kit. Lives in the hero and as a dedicated /find page. Real, interactive mock below — try it.

Behavior

Each selection enables the next. Model → match shows the matching kit inline, with photo, price, install time, and a "View Kit" button.

Fallback

Bike not found? Panel flips to "Tell us what you ride" with a short form that pings Frank directly. No dead ends.

Persistence

Selected bike saved in localStorage — every kit page, gallery filter, and pricing row reflects "your" bike for the rest of the session.
