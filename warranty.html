Your Purchase, Protected

Warranty.

Three years. Unlimited miles. Materials and workmanship. Covered from the date of purchase, straight from Pleasanton, Kansas.

3 Year · Unlimited Miles

All Frankenstein Trike rear ends and Trike Kits are covered by a three-year unlimited-miles warranty on materials and workmanship from the date of purchase.

We build every rear end and every kit right here in Pleasanton, Kansas using Dana differentials, 9” Ford street/strip axles, billet 4-piston calipers, stainless steel rotors, and grade 8 or better hardware. If a covered part fails, we stand behind it.

What's Covered

Repair or replacement of parts that fail due to defects in materials or workmanship during the warranty period.

What's Not Covered

The warranty is limited to repair or replacement of parts — not labor or paint — and does not cover any of the following:

  • Parts that fail as a result of improper installation, misuse, or abuse.
  • Parts that fail as a result of the failure of another (non-Frankenstein) part.
  • Parts installed for use on off-road vehicles, or for racing or competition use — these carry no warranty.
  • Parts modified for use, or installed for use, other than their original design and application — these carry no warranty.
  • Labor costs associated with diagnosis, removal, reinstallation, or repainting.

Swing Arm Core Charge

When applicable, kits include a core charge on the original swing arm. Please follow this policy carefully to remain eligible for your core refund:

  • Unusable, damaged, or compromised swing arms will not be accepted and will not be eligible for core refund.
  • Damaged swing arms will not be returned to the customer unless specifically requested.
  • The customer is responsible for shipping charges on the return of a damaged swing arm.
  • Package swing arms with care. Swing arms damaged during shipping will not be accepted.
  • The customer is responsible for any shipping claims related to damage in transit.

How to Start a Warranty Claim

Call our shop or send an email with your order number, motorcycle year/make/model, and a description of the issue. A product specialist will walk you through the next steps — typically an inspection, a return authorization, and a repair or replacement.
