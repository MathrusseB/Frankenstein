Our Story.

Two builders, one revolution, and three decades of hand-built trike rear ends and bolt-on conversion kits.

The History

Since 1995, owner Frank Pedersen has designed and produced award-winning choppers, custom motorcycle frames, and trikes.

In 2005, Frank and longtime friend Steinar Bergby decided the trike industry needed the next generation of rear ends. They adapted their combined experience in design, style, and fabrication to create the revolutionary Frankenstein Hot Rod Trike Rear End.

The rear end was originally conceived as a custom trike rear end that was lighter, stronger, narrower, more reliable, and better-looking than what was available. They introduced the 30” Hot Rod Trike rear end and custom bike builders loved it.

What they didn't expect was the overwhelming interest from individuals who wanted to convert their stock bikes into trikes.

So they made wider rear ends better suited to stock-bike conversions. Gradually, with each conversion, they designed swing arms, fenders, and fender brackets, and sourced wheels and tires. Within two years they had kits for most Harley-Davidson models.

Today, Frankenstein Trikes offers complete, simple, bolt-on trike kits that install in a single day — built with Dana differentials, 9” Ford street/strip axles, billet 4-piston calipers, stainless steel rotors, grade 8 hardware, and 6061 T6 billet aluminum.

They're constantly adding new products, listening to user feedback, and expanding options. The Hot Rod Billet and Light Sport Trike Kits are manufactured right here in Pleasanton, Kansas, USA.
