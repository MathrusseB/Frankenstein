## OUR STORY

Since 1995 Frankenstein Trikes owner Frank Pedersen has designed and produced award-winning Choppers, Custom motorcycle frames, and Trikes.

In 2005 Frank Pedersen and long time friend Steinar Bergby decided the Trike industry needed the next generation of Trike rear ends. Frank and Stein adapted their experience in design, style, and fabrication to create the revolutionary Frankenstein Trikes Hot Rod Trike Rear End.

The Frankenstein Trike Rear End was originally conceived to provide a custom Trike rear end that was lighter, stronger, narrower, more reliable, and better looking than what was available. They introduced the 30” Hot Rod Trike rear end and custom bike builders loved it.

What they didn’t expect was the overwhelming interest from individuals who wanted to convert their stock bike into a Trike.

As a result, they made wider rear ends that are better suited for stock bike conversions. Gradually with each conversion they designed swing arms, fenders, and fender brackets and sourced wheels and tires. Within the first two years they developed kits for most Harley-Davidson models.

Now they offer customers complete, simple, bolt-on Trike kits that can be installed in one day.

Quality and design set them apart. They use only the best components and materials such as Dana differentials, 9” Ford street/strip axles, billet 4 piston calipers, stainless steel rotors, grade 8 or better hardware, and 6061 T6 Billet aluminum.

They are constantly adding new products, listening to user feedback and expanding options. The Hot Rod Billet and Light Sport Trike Kits are manufactured right here in Pleasanton, Kansas, USA.

Pillars: Quality, Service, Value.

“Choosing a Trike kit is an important decision. We expect you to compare our Trike kits to those of other manufacturers. We believe, the more you compare, the better we look.”

## FAQ

**Q: Can I install the kit myself?**
A: Yes, all kits are designed for owner installation. General mechanical knowledge required.

**Q: Is it a straight axle?**
A: No, it is a “fixed axle” with a Dana 30 Differential (spider gears) which allows the inside wheel to turn slower while turning a corner.

**Q: Does Frankenstein Trikes offer independent suspension?**
A: No. During design they looked at pros and cons of both. Additional weight and parts increase chance of malfunction. While cornering, independent suspension tends to lean outward. Independent suspension has advantage on very rough roads, but disadvantages outweigh this. H-D Tri Glide also uses a fixed axle.

**Q: How much does the Sport Trike add to the bike?**
A: 120–150 lbs, compared to 300–700 lbs with other manufacturers.

**Q: What size tires and rims fit?**
A: Hub accepts Ford bolt pattern 5 on 4.5” with 1/2”-20 threaded studs. Suggested: 15x8 with 3 1/4” back spacing or 15x10 with 3 5/8” back spacing. Custom wheels/tires may not work with their fenders and brackets.

**Q: Do I need dual outboard brakes?**
A: Kits come standard with one caliper and rotor, providing plenty of stopping power. Outboard brakes offered for towing heavy loads.

**Q: Do I have to install raked Triple Trees?**
A: Highly recommended. They offer “POWER STEER” Triple Trees for all kits.

**Q: Would I have to change my shocks?**
A: No, most applications accept stock shocks. When they don’t, shocks are included with the kit.

**Q: Do I have to change electrical components?**
A: No, kits are designed to not affect electrical components.

**Q: Would I have to change my exhaust?**
A: Yes. Stock exhaust will not work. Short exhaust typically used. They offer exhaust solutions.

**Q: Is the trike stable in corners?**
A: Very stable with differential and car tires, but use best judgment on sharp corners.

**Q: Can I convert a shaft drive motorcycle?**
A: Not with their kit.

## INSTALLATION HINTS & TIPS

- Familiarize yourself with and inventory all components and hardware before starting.
- Center caps on wheels have a flange — install from the back of the wheel before mounting on axle.
- Recommended tire pressure: 25 PSI on 15x8 tires.
- Grease seepage from bearings is normal and will subside (bearings are often overpacked by manufacturer).
- Bearing failures in rare cases have been due to installation errors (not using supplied bolts for pulley, incorrect spacer sizing).
- Brake pad replacement instructions: Hawg Halters and MidWest caliper instructions linked as PDFs.
- Rake and trail explanation linked as PDF.
