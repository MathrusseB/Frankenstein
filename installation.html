Installation.

Every Frankenstein Trike Kit is engineered to install in a single day with common shop tools. Owner-installable, dealer-supported.

The Overview

Designed for a One-Day Install.

Our Trike Kits are designed to be a simple bolt-on installation. From the first Hot Rod rear end to today's full conversions, every part is engineered to drop onto your stock Harley-Davidson without reworking your frame, your electrical, or (in most cases) your shocks. General mechanical knowledge is all that's required.

Kits ship complete — down to the last nut and bolt — in five separate boxes, packed with expandable foam to prevent shipping damage. Unpack, inventory, install, ride.

If you'd rather skip the wrench time, any Frankenstein authorized dealer can handle the install for you. The kit is the same either way.

Before You Start

Installation Hints & Tips.

Field-tested notes from Frank and the Frankenstein tech team. Read through the full list before you turn the first bolt.

  1. Familiarize yourself with and inventory all components and hardware before starting. Most install headaches come from a missing part noticed three hours in — check the box first.
  2. Center caps on the wheels have a flange. Install the caps from the back of the wheel before mounting the wheel on the axle.
  3. Recommended tire pressure is 25 PSI on 15×8 tires. Set this before the first ride and re-check after a heat cycle.
  4. Some grease seepage from the bearings is normal and will subside with use. The manufacturer packs bearings on the heavy side — it's not a leak.
  5. Rare bearing failures have been traced to installation errors — most often not using the supplied bolts for the pulley, or using the wrong spacer size. Use the supplied hardware exactly as specified.
  6. Brake pad replacement? We've got illustrated PDFs for both the Hawg Halters and MidWest calipers — linked below.
  7. Running raked triple trees? The rake & trail explainer PDF (also below) walks through the geometry in plain English.
